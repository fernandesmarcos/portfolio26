M V S F S

MVSFS represents my full name—Marcos Vinicius da Silva Fernandes de Souza. In Brazil, lengthy names are the norm; mine is no exception. You can imagine that filling out forms comes with its share of challenges. For years, it was a problem, and I avoided even saying it in public, but now I see how unique it could be, and I want to use it somehow. Thanks, mom and dad.

I'm an Associate Design Director at Work & Co, based in Brooklyn, NY, where I lead teams in designing and delivering digital products for major clients such as Epic Games, Fox Sports, T-Mobile, Audacy, and Gatorade. My work focuses on creating products that combine simplicity with high functionality, making sure users can complete their tasks efficiently while enjoying a smooth and intuitive digital experience.

My journey in digital design began when I was just 16, in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. I was always curious about how things worked and had a passion for problem-solving. One day, I realized that websites weren't just static pages; they could actually help people with everyday tasks. From that moment, I was hooked. I started teaching myself to code and design, driven by the idea of making digital tools that improve people's lives. The early days were filled with trial and error, but it was through this exploration that I discovered my love for simplicity in design — how reducing complexity could have a big impact on user satisfaction.

After moving to Brooklyn in 2017 to continue my career at Work & Co, I deepened my approach to design. My philosophy centers on creating digital products that not only look good but also deliver real value by focusing on user needs. I believe the best designs are those that blend beauty with functionality, where aesthetics enhance the experience without getting in the way of usability.

Today, I continue to apply the same curiosity and focus on simplicity that I started with. I believe that transparency in messaging, empathy toward users, and attention to detail are key to delivering products that truly resonate with people. There's nothing more satisfying than watching users interact with a product and knowing that thoughtful design has made their experience smoother and more enjoyable.
